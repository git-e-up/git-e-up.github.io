I USED TO WORK IN A ROOM WITH A BUNCH OF WEB DEVELOPERS. THERE WAS JUST ONE PROBLEM:

I WASN'T ONE OF THEM



For the better part of the past decade, I've been employed as an accountant. At my last job, I found myself in a room full of coders, peering at their text editors, wondering how they turned that jibber jabber into beautiful websites.

After thinking about all the software I was using for accounting and HR, it occurred to me... I should be programming. As much fun as you've probably heard accounting is, it seemed like I could make a greater impact if I used my business background to build new tools that help people.

In March of this year, I left my job to attend a web bootcamp called General Assembly, and you are now seeing just the tip of the iceberg from me with this site and my class projects below. Good things are on the horizon.
